nimblejack

While at nimblejack, I was tasked with doing a near-complete overhaul of our brand. This included a new website, brand style guide, new colors, font, brand "personality", and several design elements and assets.

The existing site (and brand) looked something like this:

Old site

Our existing colors were a bit mismatched, dull and dated. Our fonts were inconsistent, and the overall look and feel of our site was not eye-catching or modern.

The old site had no clear calls to action, and was riddled with unclear copy and unmeaningful icons.

After lots of brainstorming, interviews, comparing ideas and fleshing out concepts, I ended up with this:

View the style guide

The only thing leadership would not allow me to update was our logo (aside from updating colors). You can find an alternate design I proposed used in my site design below.

Through interviewing our leadership team and asking people outside of our company about their perception of it, I came up with 5 brand personality words: 'Relational', 'Vibrant', 'Wide-eyed', 'Consultative', and 'Adaptable'.

I let these words direct the visual style of the rest of our brand.

For color, I chose a vibrant blue, red and yellow. Being the primary colors, they have a feeling of child-likeness, being the first category colors you learn about in kindergarten.

For fonts, I chose "Lato". nimblejack used a lot of client-facing documents, and to avoid inconsistencies or extra work for employees, decided to choose a single font that was free, able to be accessed across Google docs, and would work for both headers and body copy.

From there, I created some various brand elements, like the three stripes and some isometric illustrations to represent our services.

I then designed the site and built it in Wordpress.
